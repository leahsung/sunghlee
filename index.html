About Me

Welcome to my academic website. I am a Ph.D. candidate in The Media School at Indiana University, where I investigate human-AI communication and human-computer interaction/design (HCI/d). My interdisciplinary research bridges media psychology, artificial intelligence, and user experience design to understand how people interact with and perceive emerging technologies in entertainment and communication contexts.

Before pursuing my doctoral studies, I brought valuable industry experience from the Korean entertainment sector, where I worked as a theme park design project manager, strategic media-commerce experience designer for CJ Corporation and CJ ENM, focusing on user behavior analysis and storytelling. This unique combination of academic rigor and industry insight informs my research approach, allowing me to examine both theoretical frameworks and practical applications of communication technologies.

My dissertation work explores collaborative dialogue with generative AI for problem-solving, contributing to our understanding of human-machine communication processes. I am actively seeking a tenure-track position where I can continue advancing knowledge in media psychology and human-AI interaction while mentoring the next generation of scholars in communication and media studies.

Current Research Focus

My research investigates how humans and artificial intelligence systems engage in collaborative problem-solving through sustained dialogue interactions. This work contributes to both theoretical understanding of human-machine communication and practical applications in educational and professional contexts.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy centers on bridging theoretical knowledge with practical application, drawing from my unique background in both academia and industry. I believe effective media education requires students to understand both the psychological foundations of communication and the real-world contexts in which these theories apply. In my courses, I integrate insights from media psychology research with contemporary examples from digital platforms, entertainment industries, and emerging technologies like AI. I strive to create inclusive learning environments where students can critically examine media processes while developing practical skills for the evolving media landscape.
